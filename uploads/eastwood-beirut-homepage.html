What parents ask first

Safe. Seen.
Held.

Before curriculum, before outcomes — you want to know your child is safe and seen. We've built our campus around that answer, and we publish our policies.

Safety
Gated, guarded, always.
Published Child Protection Policy developed with Himaya, in line with UNICEF standards and Lebanese law. Licensed nurse and counsellor on campus. Evacuation Plan with two annual drills. Protocols reviewed annually.
Wellbeing
Mental health is built in.
Mindfulness is part of the daily curriculum from age 3. A full-time counselling team. Named adult advisor every student keeps for years.
Bullying
Protected by policy.
Clear reporting channels through the Child Protection Team. Our published Inclusion Policy protects every learner. Annual policy refresh. Staff trained during orientation and throughout the year.
Inclusion
A genuinely international community.
Inclusion support embedded in every classroom per our published Inclusion Policy. Multi-faith observance calendar. A genuinely welcoming community for Lebanese and expatriate families.

The admissions journey

From inquiry to first day.

Four steps. Rolling admissions year-round. Our Family Liaison team shepherds your family from first email to first day of school.

01
Tell us about your child
Reach out by email or phone. Share your child's current school, grade level, and what you're looking for. We'll reply with next steps and tour availability.
02
Campus visit or Zoom
A conversation with Admissions, a tour of classrooms in action, and a chance to meet the Principal. In-person or remote, whichever works for you.
03
Student assessment
Age-appropriate evaluation — playful for Early Years, academic for Diploma-level applicants. Never adversarial. Supported by a parent interview.
04
Decision and enrolment
Offer letter, welcome package, and your Family Liaison's contact. Visa letters, uniform fitting, bus routes — we handle them.
$100 non-refundable application and testing fee per family. Rolling admissions year-round. Candidates applying from outside Lebanon must certify transcripts through the Lebanese Embassy or Consulate in their country of origin.
