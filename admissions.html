How to apply

Three steps, start to finish.

From the first form to a warm welcome — here's the whole journey, with no deadlines along the way.

Step one

Send an enquiry

Complete the form below. It takes a few minutes.

Step two

Meet us

We hold an onboarding interview with parents and collect your documents. Early Years children (Nursery–KG2) have a friendly interview in English or French.

Step three

Evaluation

Students in Grades 1–12 sit a short internal evaluation. We then confirm placement and welcome you to the family.

What you'll need

A short checklist.

Have these ready when you apply. We'll guide you through anything that's still outstanding at your onboarding interview.

  • ✓Completed application form, plus the bus, medical, and photo-release forms (provided after your onboarding interview, or by email)
  • ✓Official transcripts or report cards from the last three years, including the current year (progress reports for Early Years, if applicable)
  • ✓A Ministry of Education–certified document confirming completion of the previous academic year
  • ✓A copy of the vaccination booklet (required for Grades 1–5)
  • ✓Passport photos — two for Grades 1–12, four for Early Years
  • ✓A copy of the student's passport (for IB or American pathways)
  • ✓A non-refundable $100 application and testing fee, per family
Eligibility

Who can apply.

Placement is based on age for our youngest learners, and on a short review and evaluation for everyone else.

Age by 30 September

Early Years

  • Nursery 3 years old — fully toilet-trained
  • KG1 4 years old
  • KG2 5 years old
Grades 1–12

Placement review

Placement follows a review of the last three years' report cards, a Ministry-certified completion certificate, and a short internal evaluation.

Language

English support

Students who need extra English take a placement assessment for the English as a Second Language programme.

Reviewed case by case

Neurodivergent applicants

We ask for all assessments and documentation, and the child spends two trial days with us for observation before a decision.

Good to know

A couple of important notes.

Transcripts

Applying from outside Lebanon

Students entering Grades 2–12 must have transcripts certified by the Lebanese Embassy or Consulate in the country they're leaving.

Disclosure

Full disclosure matters

Please share any prior diagnosis or support for developmental, speech, learning, social-emotional, or physical needs at the time of application — it lets us put the right support in place. If material information is withheld and later comes to light, the school may decline or withdraw enrollment, and fees already paid are non-refundable.
