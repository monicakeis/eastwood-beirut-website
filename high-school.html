The Two Paths

One school. Two routes to the world.

From Grade 7

The American Diploma.

An English-language diploma recognized in Lebanon and around the world. Broad, rigorous, and built for university anywhere. Arabic and French continue as languages, and every student sits the SAT, administered on campus.

Grades 10 and 12

The IB Diploma.

The full IB Diploma Programme. Six subjects, three at higher level, plus the Extended Essay, Theory of Knowledge, and CAS. Graduates enter university a year ahead, at sophomore standing. Eastwood was among the first schools to bring the IBDP to Lebanon.

How They Connect

One decision you do not have to make yet.

Students can move between paths. Those ready for more can step up from the American Diploma to the full IB after Grade 10. Two tracks, one high school, one standard, and one goal: the university your child deserves.
